        About Openle

        Openle is a daily chess opening puzzle. Everyone gets the same hidden opening each day — guess openings and the move tree branches where your guess stops matching the answer, showing how close you are.

        Switch to Practice for endless random puzzles across five difficulty tiers, or build your own tree in the fullscreen view. Opening data comes from Lichess; descriptions are adapted from Wikipedia.

        Made for people who love openings. Have an idea or found a bug? Send feedback.

        Frequently asked questions

        How do you play Openle?

        Type an opening as your guess. Each guess reveals how many moves deep it shares the same line as the hidden opening, and the board plays out the moves so you can see where you diverged — solve it in as few guesses as you can.

        Is Openle free?

        Yes. Openle is completely free, with no ads, no account, and no tracking.

        When does the daily puzzle reset?

        A new daily puzzle is available every day at midnight UTC. Once you finish it, switch to Practice mode for unlimited random puzzles.

        What is a chess opening tree?

        An opening tree maps how chess openings branch apart move by move from the starting position. In Openle, each guess is scored by how deep it follows the same branch as the hidden opening.

        Privacy

        Openle keeps things simple. Your progress, statistics, and preferences are stored only in your own browser (localStorage) — they stay on your device and are never sent to us.

        There are no accounts and no tracking cookies, and we don't collect personal information. We use Cloudflare Web Analytics, a privacy-friendly, cookieless service that gives us anonymous, aggregate traffic counts without tracking you across sites or building a profile.
